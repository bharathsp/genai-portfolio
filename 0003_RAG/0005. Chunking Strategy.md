# 📄✂️ Chunking Strategy in RAG

---

## 🧩 1. What is Chunking Strategy? (Simple Definition)

**Chunking Strategy** is the process of **breaking large data (documents, PDFs, etc.) into smaller meaningful pieces (chunks)** before storing and retrieving them in a RAG system.

👉 In short:
**Chunking = Splitting data into smart, searchable pieces**

---

## 💡 2. Explanation in Simple Terms

LLMs and embedding models (like those from Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence) **cannot process very large documents at once**.

So we:

1. Take a big document 📄
2. Break it into smaller parts ✂️
3. Convert each part into embeddings 🧠
4. Store them in a vector database 💾

---

👉 When a user asks a question:

* Only **relevant chunks** are retrieved 🔍
* Not the entire document

---

## 🔍 Why Chunking Matters

Without chunking:

* ❌ Too much irrelevant data
* ❌ Poor retrieval
* ❌ Context overflow

With chunking:

* ✅ Precise answers
* ✅ Faster retrieval
* ✅ Better accuracy

## ⚙️ Types of Chunking Strategies

---

### 📏 1. Fixed-Size Chunking

* Split by number of words/tokens

Example:

* Every 500 tokens

✅ Simple
❌ May break context

---

### 📖 2. Semantic Chunking

* Split based on meaning

Example:

* Paragraphs
* Sections

✅ Better understanding
✅ More natural

---

### 🔁 3. Overlapping Chunking

* Chunks overlap slightly

Example:

* Chunk 1: tokens 1–500
* Chunk 2: tokens 400–900

👉 Preserves context between chunks

---

### 🧠 4. Hierarchical Chunking

* Multi-level chunks:

  * Section → Subsection → Paragraph

👉 Useful for large structured documents

## ⚙️ 6. Requirements / Considerations

---

### 📏 1. Chunk Size Selection

* Too large ❌ → irrelevant info
* Too small ❌ → missing context

👉 Balance is key

---

### 🔁 2. Overlap Strategy

* Prevents loss of context

---

### 🧠 3. Embedding Model Compatibility

* Works with models like:

  * BAAI (BGE models)

---

### 💾 4. Vector Database

* Store chunks efficiently
* Examples:

  * Pinecone
  * Weaviate

---

### 📄 5. Data Cleaning

* Remove noise:

  * Headers
  * Footers
  * Duplicates

---

### 🧪 6. Experimentation

* No one-size-fits-all
* Tune based on:

  * Use case
  * Data type
